# Analytics Findings

## Summary

YouTube is showing MrLluminati Gaming Shorts in the Shorts feed. The core issue is not primarily tags. The main issue is that too many viewers swipe away before the clip proves itself.

## Key Findings

1. Shorts are getting Shorts Feed exposure.
2. Tags are secondary. They help classification, but they do not save weak retention.
3. Swipe-away is the main bottleneck.
4. Stayed-to-watch has often been around 32%-41%.
5. 32%-41% stayed-to-watch is not enough for a bigger Shorts push.
6. Shorts in the 10-18 second range are structurally stronger than 40-60 second Shorts for this channel.
7. Clips around 13-22 seconds with clear action/conflict have done better.
8. Stronger first-frame hooks are needed.
9. Technical titles underperform curiosity/conflict titles.
10. CS2 toxic/random teammate content is the most promising current direction.
11. Repeated self-view testing from another account should not be used to judge real reach.
12. Do not panic-delete, private, or reupload immediately just because a Short pauses at 0 views.
13. YouTube Shorts can push in test cycles, then pause if retention/swipe signals are weak.

## Retention Targets

- Minimum target: 45%+ stayed-to-watch.
- Strong target: 50%+ stayed-to-watch.
- Excellent target: 60%+ stayed-to-watch.
- For 10-14 second clips, aim for 80%+ average percentage viewed.

## What To Watch In Future Analytics

Check each Short for:

- shown in feed
- viewed vs swiped away
- average view duration
- average percentage viewed
- likes per 100 views
- comments
- subscribers gained
- first 24-hour view curve

Decision rule: if a Short gets shown but swiped away heavily, fix the hook, first frame, title, and length before blaming tags.

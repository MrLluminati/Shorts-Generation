# Shorts Strategy

Primary reference for MrLluminati Gaming Shorts. Future Codex sessions should treat this as the main rulebook.

## Current Diagnosis

- YouTube is showing the channel's Shorts in the Shorts feed.
- The main problem is not tags.
- The main problem is viewers swiping away too often.
- Stayed-to-watch has often been around 32%-41%, which is not strong enough for a larger push.
- Shorts around 10-18 seconds perform structurally better than 40-60 second Shorts.
- Clips around 13-22 seconds with clear action, conflict, or payoff have done better than slow setup clips.
- Technical titles like "Long Angle Spray" or "Clean Angle Hold" are weaker than curiosity/conflict titles.
- CS2 toxic/random teammate content is currently the most promising direction.
- Repeated self-view testing from another account should not be used to judge actual reach.
- Do not panic-delete, private, or immediately reupload Shorts just because they pause at 0 views.
- YouTube Shorts often tests a clip, pauses, and may retest later. Weak retention/swipe signals can stop the push.

## Length Rules

- Preferred length: 10-18 seconds.
- Acceptable length: 8-22 seconds.
- Avoid 30+ second Shorts unless the story is unusually strong and retention is likely.
- End fast after the payoff. Do not let a clip drift for 3-5 seconds after the moment is over.

## First-Frame Rule

The first frame must already show tension, motion, danger, conflict, or the immediate setup to the payoff.

Remove:

- menu screens
- scoreboard screens
- slow walking
- dead setup
- long driving with no danger
- waiting for enemies
- looting or inventory moments unless they directly create the payoff

Start 0.5-1.5 seconds before the action/conflict/payoff, not 5-10 seconds before.

## Retention Targets

- Minimum target: 45%+ stayed-to-watch.
- Strong target: 50%+ stayed-to-watch.
- Excellent target: 60%+ stayed-to-watch.
- For 10-14 second Shorts, aim for 80%+ average percentage viewed.
- For 15-22 second Shorts, aim for 65%-80% average percentage viewed.

## Hook Style Rules

Hooks should be emotion-first and accurate to the footage. Use 3-6 words where possible.

Good hook styles:

- betrayal: "MY TEAM TRIED TO KILL ME"
- timing: "HE PEEKED TOO LATE"
- chaos: "SOLO QUEUE IS PAIN"
- panic: "NO TIME TO RELOAD"
- payoff: "THEY PUSHED ONE BY ONE"

Avoid:

- generic labels: "CS2 Gameplay"
- technical-only labels: "Angle Hold"
- misleading hooks: calling a deer hunt a wolf escape
- overexplaining the clip in long text

## Game-Specific Strategy

### CS2

Priority pillar. Favor toxic/random teammates, solo queue chaos, team-kill attempts, timing mistakes, reload panic, quick reaction kills, multi-kill chains, and funny FPS fails.

Best clip types:

- teammate tries to team kill
- enemy peeks into a held angle
- two or more enemies push one by one
- reload timing almost fails
- fast clutch-like reaction
- sudden fail with funny audio

### Survival Horror / Zombie Games

Use clips with immediate fear, danger, sound cues, or a fast payoff.

Best clip types:

- zombie panic
- horde breach
- mutant ambush
- jumpscare
- low ammo / no escape
- claustrophobic tunnels
- survival mistake with payoff

### Minecraft Clips

Use "Minecraft clips" unless the footage is specifically modded.

Best clip types:

- creeper timing
- funny death
- unexpected mob
- cave panic
- Nether mistake
- base problem
- rare discovery

### Cinematic / Action Games

Use only instantly understandable moments. Cinematic games need a clear visual hook fast.

Best clip types:

- close calls
- crashes
- train jumps
- fast takedowns
- suspicious RP meetings
- clean landings
- action scenes that make sense without context

## Metadata Rules

Every Short package must include:

- title
- 5 alternative titles
- full description
- short description
- comma-separated tags
- targeted hashtags
- recommended description hashtags
- thumbnail/on-screen hook text
- pinned comment
- notes with source timestamps and selection reasoning

Titles should sell the emotional reason to watch, not just describe the mechanic.

## Hashtag Rules

Use 6-8 targeted hashtags per Short. Hashtags should match the actual game and event. Do not stuff unrelated trending tags.

Examples:

- CS2: `#CS2 #CounterStrike2 #CS2Shorts #SoloQueue #GamingShorts #FPSGaming`
- Survival horror: `#SurvivalHorror #ZombieGame #HorrorGaming #GamingShorts #ResidentEvil #7DaysToDie`
- Minecraft clips: `#Minecraft #MinecraftShorts #MinecraftClips #SurvivalMinecraft #GamingShorts`
- GTA RP: `#GTARP #GTA5RP #GamingShorts #Roleplay #GTAOnline`

## Quality-Control Checklist

Before final output:

- first frame is interesting
- hook appears immediately
- clip is 10-18 seconds where possible
- no dead time at the start
- no dragged ending
- title is accurate and curiosity-driven
- text does not cover crosshair, enemy, kill feed, car, or main action
- audio is usable
- video is vertical 9:16, ideally 1080x1920
- metadata and notes are complete
- content pillar is clear

## Best Pattern To Repeat

Repeat: GTA RP conflict, bad decision, police/cuffed moment, near crash, failed landing, expensive mistake, or unfair outcome.

Why it works:

- The viewer gets the situation in the first second.
- The title asks for judgment.
- The payoff is social, not just visual.
- At 25 seconds, the clip does not overstay.

Best formula:

| Time | Purpose | Example Overlay |
|---:|---|---|
| 0-1s | Consequence first | WAS I WRONG? |
| 1-4s | Show the problem immediately | COPS PULLED UP |
| 4-20s | One clean action thread | NO EXTRA DRIVING |
| 20-27s | Payoff / reaction / result | I GOT CUFFED |
| 27-30s | Clean end | COMMENT YES OR NO |

Use only 2-3 overlays per Short. Keep each overlay under 4 words.

## Worst Pattern To Stop

Stop using these as Shorts unless they have a clear crash, fight, mistake, argument, jump, landing, or payoff:

- Long scenic driving.
- "Job started" clips.
- Delivery route clips.
- Pure flying with no danger or landing payoff.
- Generic cinematic openings.
- Menu, trading, or price-check moments without a surprising result.

These can still work in long-form, but they are weak Shorts feed material because viewers need a reason to stay within the first second.

## New MrLluminati Shorts Formula

Every Short must pass this checklist before export:

1. First frame already shows conflict, danger, result, or a weird decision.
2. Title asks a question or promises a mistake/payoff.
3. Runtime is 18-28 seconds unless the retention data proves longer works.
4. No long driving before the event.
5. No looting, menus, empty route travel, or waiting.
6. No abrupt mid-sentence cuts.
7. One event only.
8. Overlay text uses short lines: 2-4 words.
9. End on the result, not after the result.
10. If the clip needs explanation, it is probably long-form, not a Short.

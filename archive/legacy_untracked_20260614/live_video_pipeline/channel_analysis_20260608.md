## Editing Strategy

The channel should treat every live video as raw material for two products:

### Short Form

Use high-event moments:

- Unexpected fail
- Argument or decision point
- Clutch survival
- Funny punishment
- Rare item or progress unlock
- Horror jump/panic moment
- Viewer-readable mistake and payoff

Output target:

- 3-8 Shorts per live video
- 20-45 seconds when possible
- 1080x1920 vertical
- Hook in first 1-2 seconds
- Captions, zooms, SFX polish, and clean audio
- Titles framed as a question, mistake, challenge, or consequence

### Long Form

Do not upload raw multi-hour lives as the main edited product. Build a watchable episode:

- 6-18 minutes for normal edits
- 20-35 minutes only when the live has strong progression
- Remove dead air, repeated grinding, setup delays, and off-topic drift
- Preserve story continuity: goal, struggle, turning point, payoff
- Add section cards only when the game/session needs structure

## Best Starting Priority

1. GTA RP lives or clips, because the best Short is already GTA RP and the format has natural conflict.
2. 7 Days to Die lives, because failures and horror survival moments already work.
3. Minecraft modpack lives, but edit them into progression arcs instead of raw grind.

## Metadata Pattern

Short titles should avoid generic hashtag-only naming. Use a readable hook first:

- `Was I Wrong For This Arrest? | GTA RP #shorts`
- `Zombie Dogs Ruined Everything | 7 Days to Die #shorts`
- `I Almost Lost The Whole Run | Minecraft Modpack #shorts`

Long-form titles should sell the session outcome:

- `This GTA RP Arrest Went Completely Wrong`

## What To Remember

Tags are not magic. YouTube says title, thumbnail, and description are more important for discovery, and tags mainly help with misspellings or alternate names. Hashtags are public discovery labels. Use both, but win through the first 2 seconds, retention, rewatchability, and comment bait.

For Shorts monetization, the hard target is 1,000 subscribers plus 10 million valid public Shorts views in the last 90 days. That means the channel needs at least one breakout clip, but it also needs a repeatable format so a viral viewer has a reason to subscribe.

## Hashtag Rule

Use 10 to 15 hashtags in the description. Do not cross 60. Do not paste hidden tags into the public description.

Best mix:
- 4 franchise tags
- 3 character or scene tags
- 3 content-format tags
- 1 brand tag
- 1 `#shorts`

## Hidden Tag Rule

Put these in YouTube Studio under `Show more > Tags`.

Best mix:
- Exact franchise name
- Alternate spellings
- Character names
- Scene-specific phrase
- Viewer search phrase
- Niche phrase
- Brand name

Keep tags relevant. Irrelevant trend stuffing can hurt the channel.
